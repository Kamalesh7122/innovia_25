About the Event

Neuro Hack is an intensive two-round hackathon where innovation meets implementation. Participants will work in teams to develop AI and ML-based solutions to real-world challenges. This event tests your creativity, technical skills, and teamwork under pressure through a comprehensive ideation-to-prototype journey.

Problem Statements

1. Smart College Bus Transportation System with Live Tracking

Develop a smart transportation management system that allows students and staff to track college buses in real time using GPS technology.

2. College Tour in Metaverse

Create a virtual metaverse experience that allows users to explore the college campus from anywhere in the world.

3. Prediction of Energy Consumption Using Machine Learning

Build a machine learning-based predictive model to analyze and forecast the college's energy consumption patterns.

4. Virtual Lab Assistant Using Voice Commands

Introduce a virtual AI-powered lab assistant that responds to voice commands for performing tasks.

5. Solar Panel Cleaning Mechanism

Design an automated solar panel cleaning system that enhances the efficiency of solar power generation.

6. Smart Sanitary Napkin Vending Machines

Develop a smart IoT-enabled vending machine for sanitary napkins within hostels.

7. Laser-Followed Seating Mechanism in Meeting Halls

Introduce an automated seating management system using laser tracking technology.

8. Staff Live Location Sharing Inside College Campus

Develop a campus-based live location tracking system for staff members.

Rules & Regulations

  • Team size: 4 members
  • Each team must select ONE problem statement from the provided list
  • Problem statements cannot be modified or replaced with custom ideas
  • First come, first served - Maximum 30 teams allowed
  • All team members must be present during both rounds
  • Use of any programming language and AI/ML frameworks is allowed
  • Internet access will be provided during the event
  • Plagiarism will lead to immediate disqualification

Judging Criteria

  • Innovation & Creativity: Unique approach to solving the problem
  • Technical Implementation: Quality of code and prototype functionality
  • Feasibility: Practicality and real-world applicability
  • Presentation: Clear explanation and communication skills
  • Impact: Potential to solve the given problem effectively
